Artificial Intelligence in Vertical Farming

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has played a critical role in transforming modern agriculture, especially in the growing field of vertical farming. Green Farm Corporation Ltd, in collaboration with Leeds Beckett University and the University of Lorraine, has undertaken a cutting-edge research project titled "Deep Learning-Driven Automation for Plant Health Monitoring and Resource Optimization in Vertical Farming" since December 2023. This initiative, led by our CTO cum COO Mr. Ngo Le Huy Hien (Jack Sage) from Green Farm Corporation Ltd as the Principal Investigator, seeks to leverage artificial intelligence (AI) to enhance plant health monitoring and optimize resource usage within vertical farming systems.

Project Overview

The project aims to address the key challenges that vertical farms face, including labor-intensive processes, inefficient resource management, and environmental control issues. By integrating AI models, IoT sensors, and automation technologies, the research aims to develop a fully automated system that improves plant health monitoring, optimizes water and nutrient usage, and reduces human intervention.

Application of AI

This research utilizes advanced deep learning models such as YOLOv9 and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks to detect plant diseases and predict water and nutrient requirements in real time. The system collects data through IoT sensors, which monitor environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and soil moisture, feeding this data into predictive models for precise resource management.

  • Real-time disease detection
  • Predictive analytics for resource optimization
  • Reinforcement learning to automate environmental control, optimizing factors like temperature and humidity to improve plant growth.

The automation will extend beyond plant health monitoring, with plans for robotic systems to automate harvesting tasks by 2026. This will further enhance the efficiency and scalability of vertical farming.

Research Leadership and Collaboration

The project is spearheaded by Principal Investigator Mr. Ngo Le Huy Hien (Jack Sage) – CTO cum COO of Green Farm Corporation Ltd. Joining the effort are several key researchers:

  • Professor Ah-Lian Kor from Leeds Beckett University, is an expert in sustainable and intelligent computing, focusing on Edge AI integration.
  • Professor Eric Rondeau from the University of Lorraine, specializes in wireless and sensor networking for environmental data collection.

Expected Outcomes

This ambitious 4-year project, which commenced in December 2023, aims to deliver a commercially viable, AI-driven vertical farming solution that will significantly reduce labor costs, optimize resource usage, and make vertical farming more accessible and scalable in urban environments. The project’s outcomes are expected to contribute to the broader adoption of sustainable agricultural practices worldwide.

This research is a pivotal step toward the future of farming, integrating AI to not only enhance productivity but also address critical environmental concerns, making vertical farming a sustainable and scalable solution for urban agriculture.

For more information on this project and its developments, stay tuned for updates from Green Farm Corporation Ltd and its research partners.

By integrating deep learning models, IoT sensors, and predictive analytics, we aim to create a fully automated, intelligent farming system that not only improves plant health monitoring and resource efficiency but also scales sustainably for urban agriculture. This research will enable us to minimize human intervention, reduce costs, and ultimately bring smarter, more sustainable food production to cities around the world.

– Ngo Le Huy Hien (Jack Sage), CTO & COO, Green Farm Corporation Ltd.
